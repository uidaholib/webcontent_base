Key things to know about Open Educational Resources

OERs Can Be

Course materials

Open textbooks

Software

Lesson plans

Games

Videos and graphics

Full courses, learning objects, tests, or any other tools, materials, or techinques that support access to knowledge.

Benefits for Faculty

  • Increases student retention by reducing costs
  • Assures academic freedom to modify or add content to your specifications
  • Extends your academic profile
  • Provides more relevant and engaging materials for your students

Benefits for Students

  • Low cost or free
  • Easy to find and access
  • More customized and relevant

Open Means the Freedom To

  • Revise: Content can be adapted, adjusted, modified or altered
  • Reuse: Content can be reused in its unaltered form
  • Remix: The original or revised content can be combined with other content to create something new
  • Retain: Users have the right to make, archive, and ‘own’ copies of the content
  • Redistribute: Copies of the content can be shared with others in its original, revised or remixed form

Some Facts

  • 65% of students report not purchasing a textbook because of its high price. [US PIRG]
  • 82% increase in College textbook prices between 2003 and 2013, approximately triple the rate of inflation in overall consumer prices. [CPI]
  • $1,298 is spent by the average student per year on books and supplies. [College Board]
  • A+ Studies at Virginia State University and Houston Community College found that students who used open textbooks tended to have higher grades & lower withdrawal rates than their peers who used traditional textbooks. [Hilton 2012][Feldstein 2012]
