It's Open Access Week!

Open access literature is digital, online, free of charge, and free of most copyright and licensing restrictions. In most fields, journals do not pay authors for their work yet make money off of their research while removing all rights the author had to their work. The open access model tries to correct this by creating journals that do not strip authors of their rights while still publishing quality, peer-reviewed research and by educating authors about their options in publishing. You can read an overview of open access by Peter Suber here, or interact with the resources below. For more information feel free to contact Annie Gaines.
