Open Education Week 2016 Events

Monday, April 18th

Open Textbook Petting Zoo

Library 1st floor, 10:00 – 4:00pm

Stop by for an opportunity to interact with a variety of open educational resources in different formats.

Tuesday, April 19th

Access from DAY ONE: How OpenStax free textbooks can remove barriers for you and your students

Library Classroom, 212A, 10:30 – 11:30am

A live webinar hosted by OpenStax, Rice University’s open education initiative. OpenStax provides free, immediate access to peer-reviewed textbooks for your courses. During this presentation Nicole Finkbeiner will address methods for overcoming barriers to student success, demonstrate how to access the textbooks and faculty-only resources, and answer any questions you have about the books or the initiative.

Wednesday, April 20th

Wikipedia Edit-a-thon

Library Classroom, 212A, 10:00 – 12:00pm

Join us to fact-check and enhance Wikipedia articles related to the University of Idaho by using resources at the Library! The event is open to the public and no experience is necessary--we will help you get started! We will have an overview of Wiki editing, library resources and suggested topics on hand. For more information visit our official Meetup page.

Thursday, April 21th

Panel Discussion - Faculty and Student Experiences with Open Textbooks

Library 1st Floor, 1:30 – 2:30pm

This panel discussion among faculty, students, and librarians will focus on their experiences using open textbooks or open educational resources in their courses. Renae Shrum and Brian Dennis, two faculty members already using open textbooks in their Statistics courses, will be participating.
