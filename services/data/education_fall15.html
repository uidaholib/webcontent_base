Data & Digital Services Workshops

Data & Digital Services Workshops are designed to promote awareness, build skills, and educate the University of Idaho campus community on issues associated with research and learning in the information age.

Order Date Title Description Location Lead(s)
1 Oct 7, 2015 1:30-3:00pm Web GIS - Making Your Data Available as Maps, Apps, & Layers This presentation will provide an introduction to ArcGIS Online, a web-based collaborative platform that allows users to create, manage, store, and share maps, applications, and data layers. ArcGIS Online is available to faculty, staff, and students for research, teaching, and administrative purposes. Attendees will leave the presentation with a broad understanding of the capabilities of ArcGIS Online enabling them to determine if it is useful for their needs. Intended for: students, faculty, and staff who have an interest in sharing geographic data (publically or among a private group). Click to Register Library Rm 212a Bruce Godfrey
2 Oct 14, 2015 2:30-4:00pm Clean Your Data: getting started with OpenRefine No one likes dirty data! Before analysis comes the messy work of evaluating, cleaning, and transforming data. This hands on workshop will introduce a free powertool to get the job done: OpenRefine (http://openrefine.org/). We will install Refine, create a project, and get oriented to the many features for exploring and transforming tabular data. Intended for: students, faculty, and staff with an interest in cleaning, transforming, and working data. Attendees are encouraged to bring a laptop to install OpenRefine. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Evan Williamson
3 Oct 15, 2015 3:00-4:00pm SciFinder Training Sessions Learn about search techniques for SciFinder, one of the most powerful tools for finding chemical information. Search techniques will include structure searching and reaction searching. Please feel free to bring your questions and challenges with using SciFinder! Library Rm 212a Kevin Seguin (Chemical Abstracts Service)
4 Oct 21, 2015 3:00-4:00pm Managing and Cultivating a Digital Media Presence as an Academic Are you aware of your online and digital presence? What do you find when you Google yourself – is it what you want to see? What will other people, including hiring committees, make of you after encountering the digital version of you? There is no way to avoid leaving a digital footprint these days, and many current faculty have embraced the multitude of social media platforms (Twitter, Facebook, blogs, etc.) to share their research, their thoughts, and their personal opinions. But what is the best advice for grad students and junior faculty members when it comes to managing and cultivating a professional and helpful digital presence? Join us for a discussion and demonstration of practical social media tools and resources for an active digital presence. This workshop will encourage participants to engage with social media, so please bring laptops, tablets, smart phones, etc. to the event. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Anne Gaines
5 Oct 28, 2015 3:00-4:30pm Citation Management Software Do you email articles to yourself on a regular basis? Or do you use a Google doc to keep a running list of interesting books, articles and websites? There is a better way to manage your citations! Come learn about the free, web-based citation managers: Zotero, Endnote Web and Mendeley. You can use citation managers to quickly and easily create ordered lists of what you find online or in the library. Use them for research or for your personal life! Once you master the use of a citation manager, your life will be that much more organized. The workshop will run as follows: Zotero (3:00-3:30), Endnote Web (3:30-4:00), Mendeley (4:00-4:30). Feel free to come to all three presentations or just drop in! Participants will have the opportunity to set up their own free citation manager accounts with the help of the workshop organizers, Rick and Annah. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Annah Hackett Rick Stoddart
6 Nov 4, 2015 11:30am-1:00pm Web GIS – Publishing Your Data as Web Services This presentation will provide an overview of publishing web service using ArcGIS for Server & ArcGIS Online. Attendees will learn about several different web service types and learn how to publish web services from ArcGIS for Desktop. Attendees will leave the presentation knowing how to publish a web map service. Intended for: students, faculty, and staff who are familiar with ArcGIS for Desktop. Attendees are encouraged to bring a laptop with ArcGIS for Desktop installed. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Bruce Godfrey
7 Nov 11, 2015 3:00-4:30pm Solve Problems, Save Time: Making Data Management Work for You (I) Data management planning is an increasingly useful skill for improving research process efficiency and meeting obligations of funding agencies. During these two sessions, we will focus on developing NSF-style data management plans. Among the topics addressed will be thinking about how to introduce data management activities into the research workflow, how to document and describe your research products, share them in repositories, and get credit for your hard work with digital object identifiers (DOIs). Additionally, we will cover how to address tricky questions such as long-term storage, archiving, and dissemination strategies. Attendees should plan to come away with a data management plan draft for their own current or proposed research. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Marisa Guarinello Jeremy Kenyon
8 Nov 12, 2015 3:00-4:00pm Introduction to Qualtrics Survey Software Survey much? Come learn about Qualtrics a power online survey tool suitable for research and data-gathering. Qualtrics is available to University of Idaho staff, faculty, and students. This session will be an introduction to Qualtrics and will provide a basic overview of Qualtrics features including how to create a basic survey and the different question types. We will learn about skips and display logic. We will also cover some different options for distributing survey and reporting out. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Rick Stoddart

10 Dec 2, 2015 11:30am-1:00pm So Long ArcMap, Say Hello to Pro This presentation will provide attendees with an overview of ArcGIS Pro, a new desktop application from Esri that was released earlier this year. ArcGIS Pro is a 64-bit, multithreaded application that offers a new licensing model, multiple layouts, 2D and 3D editing and more. Attendees will leave the presentation with a understanding of how to license the software and how to get started using ArcGIS Pro. Intended for: students, faculty, and staff who are familiar with ArcGIS for Desktop. Click to Register Library Rm 212a Bruce Godfrey
